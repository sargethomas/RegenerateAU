TEMPERATE GRASSLANDS

Desertification, changing weather patterns, and increased fire risks are the biggest threats that the food industry is posing to the temperate grasslands

Desertification is a form of land degradation that is negatively impacting the temperate grasslands, most severely, in Africa, and causing a scarcity in the already limited vegetation, making survival difficult for the native animals. The desertification process is caused by immoral and unsustainable agricultural processes that are used to see the easiest possible harvest rather than the most sustainable one. Using the (already scarce) arable land irresponsibly is rendering it unusable, and desertifying it, drying out and eroding the soil.

This biome is also now subject to extremely erratic and unpredictable weather patterns as greenhouse gases cause rising temperatures. Precipitation is scarce, and there are long, dry heat spells that make the lands arid and extremely susceptible to fires, which heat the environment further, as well as wiping out the wildlife that inhabits them.

Most Notable Temperate Grasslands:

  • Eurasian steppes
  • North American prairies
  • Argentine pampas
